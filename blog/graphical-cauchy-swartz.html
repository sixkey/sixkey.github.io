Partial counts

Let \(G\) be a graph together with an embedding into \(A\), i.e., an injective mapping which preserves both the edge and the non-edge relation. We say that such \(A\) is \(G\)-labeled. Recall that we denote by \(h(A, H)\) the number of homomorphisms from \(A\) to \(H\) and compute it by

\[\begin{equation} \begin{aligned} h(A, H) = \sum_{\varphi : VA \to VH}{\chi_{\varphi}(EA)}, \end{aligned} \end{equation}\]

where \(\chi_{\varphi}\) is a function which returns 1 if the image of the edges under the input mapping map to edges of \(H\) and 0 otherwise.

\[\begin{equation} \begin{aligned} h(A, H) &= \sum_{\varphi : VG \to VH}\chi_{\psi}(EG)\sum_{\psi : VA \to VH, \psi \ge \varphi}{\chi_{\varphi}(EA - EG)}, \\ &= \sum_{\varphi : VG \to VH}\chi_{\psi}(EG)h_\psi(A - G, H) \end{aligned} \end{equation}\]
